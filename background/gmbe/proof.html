GMBE Proof

The Induction Hypothesis

Based on the motivation in (TODO: add section) we hypothesize that the GMBE for a system fragmented into \(m\) fragments can be written in a form designed to mimic a corresponding MBE via:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\e}[1]{\mathcal{E}_{#1}} \newcommand{\De}[1]{\Delta\e{#1}} \newcommand{\mCk}[2]{_{#1}C_{#2}} \newcommand{\kmers}[1]{\mathbb{F}^{\left(m, #1\right)}} \newcommand{\kmeri}[2]{F^{\left(#1\right)}_{#2}} \newcommand{\ei}{\e{I}} \newcommand{\Deij}{\De{IJ}} \newcommand{\mCn}{\mCk{m}{n}} \newcommand{\nmers}{\kmers{n}} \newcommand{\nmeri}{\kmeri{n}{I}}\\\begin{split}E =& \sum_{I=1}^{m} \ei + \sum_{I=1}^{m-1}\sum_{J=I+1}^{m} \Deij + \cdots + \De{IJK\cdots m}\\ =& \sum_{\kmeri{1}{K}\in\kmers{1}} \e{\kmeri{1}{K}} + \sum_{n=2}^{m}\sum_{\nmeri\in\nmers}^{\mCn} \De{\nmeri}\end{split}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

Here the intersection-corrected energy of the \(I\)-th \(n\)-mer are defined by:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\nmersuptoi}{\mathbb{F}^{\left(m,n\right)}[1:I-1]} \newcommand{\powersetk}[2]{\mathbb{P}^{\left(#1\right)}\left(#2\right)} \newcommand{\setint}[1]{\left(\bigcap {#1}\right)} \newcommand{\nmerj}{\kmeri{n}{J}}\\\begin{split}\e{\nmeri} =& E_{\nmeri} - \sum_{\nmerj\in\nmersuptoi} E_{\nmerj\cap\nmeri} + \sum_{x\in\powersetk{2}{\nmersuptoi}} E_{\setint{x}\cap\nmeri} - \cdots +\\ & (-1) ^{I-1} E_{\setint{\nmersuptoi}\cap\nmeri}\\ =& E_{\nmeri} - \sum_{k=1}^{I-1}\sum_{x\in\powersetk{k}{\nmersuptoi}} (-1) ^ k E_{\left(\bigcap x\right)\cap \nmeri}\end{split}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

The notation is complex, but the idea is simple. The \(I\)-th \(n\)-mer’s intersection-corrected enegy is obtaind by subtracting from the \(I\)-th \(n\)-mer’s energy (the \(k=0\) term) the components of the energy which appeared in any of the previous \(I-1\) \(n\)-mers (these corrections are the \(k=1\) terms); however, for components which appear in three or more of the previous \(n\)-mers we have now removed them too many times and need to add them back in (the \(k=2\) terms). This process repeats until we have addresed the intersection of all \(I\) \(n\)-mers. If it helps you can think of this as the set-equivalent of Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization.

Akin to the MBE we obtain \(n\)-body interactions by removing \(k\)-body interactions (\(k\) less than \(n\)). The caveat here is that intersection-corrected eneriges have already done some of this for us and we need to be careful not to remove lower-order interactions too many times. To that end we define the set:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\psetp}[2]{\mathbb{P}'^{\left(#1\right)}\left(#2\right)}\\\psetp{k}{\nmeri} = \powersetk{k}{\nmeri}\setminus \left(\bigcup_{J=1}^{I-1}\psetp{k}{\nmerj}\right)\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

which is the set of \(k\)-mers that can be formed from the monomers comprising the \(I\)-th \(n\)-mer, but can not be formed from any of the previously considered \(n\)-mers. In terms of this set, the intersection-corrected interactions are:

\[\De{\nmeri} = \e{\nmeri} - \sum_{k=2}^{n-1}\sum_{\kmeri{k}{J}\in\psetp{k}{\nmeri}} \De{\kmeri{k}{J}} - \sum_{\kmeri{1}{K}\in\psetp{1}{\nmeri}} \e{\kmeri{1}{K}}\]

Proof Of Exactness

We now proceed to show that this form of the GMBE is exact. To that end consider the GMBE for \(m\) fragments:

\[E = \sum_{I=1}^{m} \ei + \sum_{n=2}^{m}\sum_{\nmeri\in\nmers}^{\mCn} \De{\nmeri}\]

We expand the \(n=m\) interaction:

\[\De{\kmeri{m}{1}} = \e{\kmeri{m}{1}} - \sum_{n=2}^{m-1}\sum_{\nmeri\in\psetp{k}{\kmeri{m}{1}}} \De{\nmeri} - \sum_{\kmeri{1}{K}\in\psetp{1}{\kmeri{m}{1}}} \e{\kmeri{1}{K}}\]

Here the notation \(F^{\left(m\right)}_{0}\) is meant to make explicit that there is only one \(m\)-mer, the entire system. Consequently, for a given \(k\) (including \(k=1\)) we have:

\[\psetp{k}{\kmeri{m}{1}} = \powersetk{k}{\kmeri{m}{1}} = \kmers{k}\]

which is to say that since there are no previous \(m\)-mers the primed powerset reduces to the normal powerset, which in turn reduces to the set of \(k\)-mers because the \(m\)-mer is the entire system. With this realization:

\[\De{\kmeri{m}{1}} = \e{\kmeri{m}{1}} - \sum_{n=2}^{m-1}\sum_{\nmeri\in\nmers} \De{\nmeri} - \sum_{\kmeri{1}{K}\in\nmers} \e{\kmeri{1}{K}}\]

and:

\[\begin{split}E =& \sum_{\kmeri{1}{K}\in\kmers{1}} \e{\kmeri{1}{K}} + \sum_{n=2}^{m-1}\sum_{\nmeri\in\nmers}^{\mCn} \De{\nmeri} + \e{\kmeri{m}{1}} - \sum_{n=2}^{m-1}\sum_{\nmeri\in\nmers} \De{\nmeri} - \sum_{\kmeri{1}{K}\in\kmers{1}} \e{\kmeri{1}{K}}\\ =& \e{\kmeri{m}{1}}\\ =& E_{\kmeri{m}{1}}\end{split}\]

which with the last line follows from the definition of the intersection-corrected \(m\)-mer energy. Basically this proof shows that, like the normal MBE, the GMBE is just a very creative way of adding zero.

Exactness of the Intersection-Corrected Energies

The exactness proof only relies on the fact that:

\[E = \e{\kmeri{m}{1}} = E_{\kmeri{m}{1}}\]

and:

\[\De{\kmeri{m}{1}} = \e{\kmeri{m}{1}} - \sum_{n=2}^{m-1}\sum_{\nmeri\in\nmers} \De{\nmeri} - \sum_{\kmeri{1}{K}\in\nmers} \e{\kmeri{1}{K}}\]

The defitions of the intersection-corrected energies and interactions of orders \(k\neq m\) are immaterial in the exactness proof. Here we show that our intersection-corrected energies are defined such that the \(n\)-body approximation to the energy of the supersystem is given by:

\[E^{(m,n)} = \sum_{\nmeri}\e{\nmeri}\]

The base case

For a system comprsied of a single \(n\)-mer we have:

\[E^{(1,n)} = E_{\kmeri{n}{1}}\]

For arbitrary \(n\), it is always true that for the \(I=1\)-th \(n\)-mer:

\[\sum_{k=1}^{I-1}\sum_{\kmeri{k}{J}\in\powersetk{k}{\nmersuptoi}} (-1)^k E_{\left(\bigcap \kmeri{k}{J}\right)\cap \nmeri} = 0\]

Hence:

\[\begin{split}E^{(1,n)} =& E_{\kmeri{n}{1}} + \sum_{k=1}^{I-1}\sum_{\kmeri{k}{J}\in\powersetk{k}{\nmersuptoi}} (-1)^k E_{\left(\bigcap \kmeri{k}{J}\right)\cap \nmeri}\\ =& \e{\kmeri{n}{1}}\end{split}\]

Proving that the proposed intersection-corrected energy formula does not under/overcount a system comprised of a single \(n\)-mer. In the next section we use induction to prove that this is true for an arbitrary number of \(n\)-mers.

Proof By Induction

We now assume that for \(\ell - 1\) \(n\)-mers the following is true:

\[E^{(\ell - 1,n)} = \sum_{I=1}^{\ell - 1}\e{\kmeri{n}{I}}\]

It is perhaps a somewhat esoteric point, but the above equation simply states that \(E^{(\ell - 1, n)}\) is the under-/over-counting free approximation to the system’s energy resulting from using \(\ell - 1\) \(n\)-mers. It is not clamining that this is a good approximation to the system’s energy, nor does it have to be for our current purposes. For example, and simplicity, let \(m\) be 2, \(\ell\) be 2, and \(n\) be 1. In this example, unless the first fragment is actually the entire system, \(E^{(\ell - 1, n)}\) will be missing energies of entire atoms (specifically the atoms that appear in the second fragment, but not the first), making it a very poor approximation to the system’s energy. In practice, the only \(\ell\) we care about is when \(\ell\) equals “\(m\) choose \(n\)”; this proof shows that the route we take to get to “\(m\) choose \(n\)” is under-/over-counting free.

Esoteric point aside, we proceed by creating the ordered set \(\mathbb{F}^{\left(m,n\right)}[1:\ell]\) by appending the \(\ell\)-th \(n\)-mer onto to the ordered set \(\mathbb{F}^{\left(m,n\right)}[1:\ell-1]\). The approximate energy is then given by IEP. We choose to write the IEP as the sum of the \(\ell-1\) approximate energy (which is intersection corrected by assumption) plus the energy of the \(\ell\)-th \(n\)-mer, corrected for the intersections of the \(\ell\)-th \(n\)-mer with the proceeding \(\ell-1\) fragments:

\[\begin{split}E^{(\ell, n)} =& E^{(\ell - 1, n)} + E_{\kmeri{n}{\ell}} - \sum_{I=1}^{\ell-1} E_{\kmeri{n}{I}\cap\kmeri{n}{\ell}} + \sum_{I=1}^{\ell-2}\sum_{J=I+1}^{\ell-1} E_{\kmeri{n}{I}\cap\kmeri{n}{J}\cap\kmeri{n}{\ell}} - \cdots\\ =& E^{(\ell-1, n)} + E_{\kmeri{n}{\ell}} - \sum_{k=1}^{\ell-1} \sum_{\kmeri{k}{I}\in \powersetk{k}{\mathbb{F}^{\left(m,n\right)}[1:\ell - 1]}} (-1)^k E_{\left(\bigcap \kmeri{k}{I}\right)\cap\kmeri{n}{\ell}}\\ =& E^{(\ell-1, n)} + \e{\kmeri{n}{\ell}}\\ =& \sum_{I=1}^{\ell}\e{\kmeri{n}{I}}\end{split}\]

Proving it for \(\ell\) \(n\)-mers.

Exactness of the Many-Body Interactions

The next step in the proof is to show that the when the GMBE is truncated at an \(n\) not equal to \(m\) (we already showed \(n\) equal to \(m\)) the intersection-corrected many-body interactions also do not over-/under-count interactions. Truncated at order \(n\) the GMBE reads:

\[E^{(m,n)} = \sum_{\kmeri{1}{I}\in\kmers{1}} \e{\kmeri{1}{I}} + \sum_{\ell=2}^{n}\sum_{\kmeri{\ell}{J}\in\kmers{\ell}}^{\mCk{m}{\ell}} \De{\kmeri{\ell}{J}}\]

Expanding the the \(n\)-body intersection-corrected interaction:

\[\begin{split}E^{(m, n)} =& \sum_{\kmeri{1}{I}\in\kmers{1}} \e{\kmeri{1}{I}} + \sum_{\ell=2}^{n-1} \sum_{\kmeri{\ell}{J}\in\kmers{\ell}}^{\mCk{m}{\ell}} \De{\kmeri{\ell}{J}} + \\ & \sum_{\kmeri{n}{J}\in\kmers{n}}^{\mCn} \left[ \e{\kmeri{n}{J}} - \sum_{\ell=2}^{n-1} \sum_{\kmeri{\ell}{K}\in\psetp{\ell}{\kmeri{n}{J}}} \De{\kmeri{\ell}{K}} - \sum_{\kmeri{1}{\ell}\in\psetp{1}{\kmeri{n}{J}}} \e{\kmeri{1}{\ell}} \right]\end{split}\]

Assuming every fragment shows up in at least one \(n\)-mer, we have:

\[\sum_{\kmeri{n}{J}\in\kmers{n}}^{\mCn} \sum_{\ell=2}^{n-1} \sum_{\kmeri{\ell}{K}\in\psetp{\ell}{\kmeri{n}{J}}} \De{\kmeri{\ell}{K}} = \sum_{\ell=2}^{n-1} \sum_{\kmeri{\ell}{K}\in\kmers{\ell}}^{\mCk{m}{\ell}} \De{\kmeri{\ell}{K}}\]

Similarly:

\[\sum_{\kmeri{n}{J}\in\kmers{n}}^{\mCn} \sum_{\kmeri{1}{\ell}\in\psetp{1}{\kmeri{n}{J}}} \e{\kmeri{1}{\ell}} = \sum_{\kmeri{1}{\ell}\in\kmers{1}}\e{\kmeri{1}{\ell}}\]

and we have:

\[E^{(m, n)} = \sum_{\kmeri{n}{J}\in\kmers{n}}^{\mCn} \e{\kmeri{n}{J}}\]

which is just the IEP for \(n\)-mers.

Equations

For a system broken into \(n\), possibly intersecting, fragments the GMBE provides a many-body decomposition of the total energy, \(E\):

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\etot}{E} \newcommand{\e}[1]{\mathcal{E}_{#1}} \newcommand{\De}[1]{\Delta\e{#1}} \newcommand{\eI}{\e{I}} \newcommand{\DeIJ}{\De{IJ}}\\E = \sum_{I}^n \ei + \sum_{IJ}^{{_n}C_2} \DeIJ + \cdots + \Delta\mathcal{E}_{IJ\cdots N}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

where:

  • \(I\), \(J\), \(K\) index fragments/monomers,

  • \(IJ\) is the dimer formed by taking the union of monomers \(I\) and \(J\),

  • the ellipses ellide terms involving trimers, tetramers, and so on up to the \((n-1)\)-mers, and

  • \(IJ\cdots N\) is the one \(n\)-mer, i.e. the entire system.

The energies and intersections appearing in the expansion are “intersection-corrected”, meaning they in general contain additional terms beyond their normal MBE-counterparts to avoid over/under correcting intersections. The intersection-corrected energies are given in terms of the non-

We use the convention that \(E_{I}\), \(E_{J}\), \(E_{K}\), etc. are the energies of the fragments and \(E_{IJ}\), \(E_{IK}\), \(E_{JK}\), \(E_{IJK}\), etc are the energies of the nmers

The proof of these equations is somewhat involved and is presented on another page gmbe-proof.

Three-Body Equation

The three-body equation is:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\eijk}{E_{IJK}}\\\etot = \eijk\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

Adding and subtracting the three-fragment, two-body expansion:

\[\etot \approx \eijk + \left(\eI + \ej + \ek + \De{IJ} + \De{IK} + \De{JK}\right) - \left(\eI + \ej + \ek + \De{IJ} + \De{IK} + \De{JK}\right)\]

Defining:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\Deijk}{\Delta E_{IJK}}\\\Deijk = \eijk - \left(\eI + \ej + \ek + \De{IJ} + \De{IK} + \De{JK}\right)\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

The three-body energy equation becomes:

\[\etot \approx \eI + \ej + \ek + \De{IJ} + \De{IK} + \De{JK}\]

It is worth noting that if we relabel dimer \(IJ\) with a single letter index \(L\), dimer \(IK\) as \(M\), and \(JK\) as \(N\), the two-body approximation looks like the one-body approximation:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\El}{E_L} \newcommand{\Em}{E_M} \newcommand{\En}{E_N} \newcommand{\Ek}{E_K} \newcommand{\Elcm}{E_{L\cap M}} \newcommand{\Elcn}{E_{L\cap N}} \newcommand{\Emcn}{E_{M\cap N}} \newcommand{\Elcmcn}{E_{L\cap M\cap N}}\\\etot \approx \El + \Em + \En - \Elcm - \Elcn - \Emcn + \Elcmcn\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

which was the idea used in the original GMBE paper to derive equations.

Noting that intersection distributes over union we have:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned} \newcommand{\Eijck}{E_{I\cup(J\cap K)}}\\\begin{split} \Eijcik =& E_{(I\cap IK) \cup (J\cap IK)}\\ =& E_{I \cup (J\cap IK)}\\ =& E_{I \cup (J\cap I) \cup (J\cap K)}\\ =& \Eijck\end{split}\\Similar manipulations afford:\\.. math::\\ \newcommand{\Ejick}{E_{J\cup(I\cap K)}} \newcommand{\Ekicj}{E_{K\cup(I\cap J)}}\\\begin{split} \Eijcjk =& \Ejick \\ \Eikcjk =& \Ekicj\end{split}\\For the triple intersection we get:\\.. math::\\ \newcommand{\tripleint}{E_{(I\cap J)\cup (I\cap K)\cup (J\cap K)}}\\\begin{split} \Eijcikcjk =& E_{(IJ \cap IK)\cap JK}\\ =& E_{[I\cup (J\cap K)]\cap JK}\\ =& E_{(I\cap JK)\cup[(J\cap K)\cap JK)]}\\ =& \tripleint\end{split}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

Generalized Energy Based Fragmentation (GEBF)

Development of GEBF was motivated by a need to treat large macromolecules/clusters that are charged or highly polar 15. The original GEBF method consists of a fragmentation method, a screening method, and an embedding method. GEBF’s original presentation also includes an energy equation which is simply a one-body GMBE corrected for the self-interaction of the point charges (i.e., it is assumed that the energies of the fragments include not only the interaction of the fragment with the point charges, but also the interactions among the point charges). Initial applications of GEBF focused on peptides and water clusters with the HF and MP2 methods. In addition to absolute energies this study also considered dipole moments and static polarizabilities.

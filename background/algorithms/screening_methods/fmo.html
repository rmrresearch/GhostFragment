FMO Screening Procedures

FMO00 Screening

The orginal FMO procedure Nakano et al.17 for screening dimers involved distance cutoffs. If the shortest inter-atom distance between two monomers \(I\) and \(J\) is less than a threshold (default is 10 Bohr), then the dimer energy is approximated by:

\[ \begin{align}\begin{aligned}\newcommand{\vr}{\vec{r}} \newcommand{\vrp}{\vec{r}'} \newcommand{\density}[2]{rho_{#1}\left(#2\right)}\\E_{IJ} = E_{I} + E_{J} - \int d\vr\int\vrp \frac{\density{I}{\vr}\density{J}{\vrp}} {\mid \vr -\vrp\mid}\end{aligned}\end{align} \]

FMO04 Screening

In the original formulation of three-body FMO , the FMO00 screening procedure is extended to three-body terms. Instead of one shortest inter-atomic distance we now have three, one for each pair of monomers. The FMO04 screening method screens out a trimer if the second smallest distance is greater than a threshold (default is 2.0, in units of van der Waal radii).
